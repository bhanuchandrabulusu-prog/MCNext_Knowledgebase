In this article, I’d like to share a summary of the Marketing Keynote from Dreamforce 2025.

One of the most striking moments was the introduction of a new term — **“Agentforce Marketing”.** Right as Dreamforce began, the official Marketing Cloud pages on X (formerly Twitter) and LinkedIn were rebranded to this new name, Agentforce Marketing — a symbolic shift.

Until now, the term “Marketing Cloud” has often been used as a synonym for “marketing” itself — more as a concept than a product name. This year, it seems that Marketing Cloud has been absorbed into a broader movement — Agentforce. That’s essentially what Agentforce Marketing represents.

For those who haven’t yet incorporated AI into their work, the term may feel a bit unfamiliar or even distant. However, looking ahead five or ten years, it’s clear that our world will be filled with AI. From that perspective, Salesforce’s decision to make a clear shift toward AI at this moment feels like a bold and correct step forward. We, too, need to adapt to this new era where **Agentforce = the use of AI.**

Salesforce, for its part, seems to be preparing systems that make Agentforce easier to use and more accessible for everyone.

**Three Key Messages from Salesforce**

- **No cost to access** — Get access at no cost, then pay as you grow.
- **No migration** — Integrated experience with Engagement & Account Engagement.
- **No one left behind** — Available to all Marketing Cloud customers.

These guiding principles will likely be detailed further in upcoming announcements. All three are inspiring directions that users will surely appreciate — but the last one, “**No one left behind**”, comes with a bit of concern.

While access to the features may indeed be available to everyone, effectively leveraging AI requires well-prepared **data**, as well as **knowledge and skills.**

Without these resources, some users might still end up being “left behind”.  
Even for Salesforce partners like us, there are parts of this transition that feel uncertain and challenging.

That’s why I believe overcoming this sense of uncertainty will become one of Salesforce’s most important themes in the years ahead. As for me, I’ll continue doing my best to share insights and updates so that none of you are left behind.

### **The Five Pillars Highlighted in the Keynote**

The Marketing Keynote centered around five key pillars, each thoughtfully explained with live demonstrations.

- **① The end of “do not reply” marketing**
- **② Journeys powered by unified data**
- **③ Experiences that adapt with customers**
- **④ Campaigns built at the speed of creativity**
- **⑤ Insights that turn into impact**

### Two-Way Email Interaction

Among these, what stood out most to me was the demonstration of **two-way email communication**, marking the end of “no-reply” marketing.

It felt as if the inbox itself had turned into an **Agentforce chat interface** — a completely new and refreshing experience.

Here’s how the demo unfolded:

**1. The recipient clicks “Reply”.**

**2. In the reply email**, the recipient asks a question — for example:  
“What’s the difference between Marketing Cloud Next and Marketing Cloud Engagement?”

**3. Almost instantly, AI notifies** the user that a new message has been prepared. The recipient then clicks the **“Show”** button.

**4. A response appears immediately** on the screen — not as plain text, but as a **beautifully formatted HTML email**, clearly organized in tables for readability.

**5. From there, the recipient can even book a 1:1 training session** directly — seamlessly completing the interaction within the same flow.

**6. Behind the scenes**, everything seemed to be orchestrated through **Flow**. Each “reply” event served as a trigger to send the next email, using a structure built with **“Wait Until Event”.** If you’re familiar with Flow or Journey Builder, that concept might immediately click for you.

### **Integration with Third-Party Products**

Another exciting aspect was the list of **extensions within Content Builder**, showcased in the demo by Indeed.

For example, tools like **Typeface** will soon be available, allowing users to **generate images automatically** directly from this interface. Many other third-party products were also listed, and it’s exciting to imagine how each of these can be leveraged in the near future.

For the full set of demonstrations, I highly recommend checking them out on **Salesforce+**. The “not-too-distant future” is already packed into these demos, so it’s definitely worth seeing for yourself.

### **Extra Insight**

Here’s some additional information: **Slack is transforming into an “Agentic OS”.** This evolution extends to the marketing domain as well, and in the future, marketing tools will be integrated on this new platform.

A concept diagram of this transformation was also shared.

Before Dreamforce, Agentforce felt like just one of many Salesforce tools, but its positioning has now dramatically shifted — it has become a **central element of Salesforce**.

We are entering **the era of Agentforce**. While knowledge of marketing itself remains important, **the ability to naturally leverage AI** will become increasingly essential. Let’s gradually get accustomed to it and prepare to maximize the power that this new era offers.
