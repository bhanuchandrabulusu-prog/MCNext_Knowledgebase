Salesforce’s annual event, Connections 2026, officially kicked off today in Chicago, USA, at 12:00 AM JST on June 4, 2026.

It was exactly one year ago at Connections 2025 that Marketing Cloud Next was first introduced in a major way. Over the past year, Marketing Cloud Next has evolved significantly through its integration with Agentforce and Data 360, becoming a next-generation marketing platform centered around AI.

At this year’s Connections, Salesforce unveiled numerous new capabilities and a bold vision designed to accelerate that evolution even further.

One message that stood out throughout the keynote was **“The Age of Marketing Makers”.** To us, it represents a shift from an era where marketers alone execute marketing activities to one where humans and AI agents collaborate to create and run marketing together.

## The Arrival of the Age of Marketing Makers

Throughout the Connections 2026 keynote, Salesforce repeatedly emphasized the concept of the **“Age of Marketing Makers”.**

The future envisioned by Salesforce is not one where marketers work alone. **Instead, it is a world where humans and AI agents work together to execute marketing at scale**.

To illustrate this transformation, Salesforce highlighted several challenges facing modern marketing and the direction organizations must take moving forward.

Traditionally, marketers analyzed data, made decisions, created content, and executed campaigns themselves.

However, as **customer data continues to grow**, **channels multiply**, and **expectations for personalization** continue to rise, it is becoming increasingly difficult for humans alone to keep pace.

Salesforce described four major shifts that are necessary:

1. **From a world where people use data to a world where data drives AI agents**
2. **From limited personalization to AI-powered personalization at scale**
3. **From one-way communications to conversational marketing**
4. **From manual work to AI-augmented capabilities**

In other words, Salesforce is envisioning a transition from a world where humans execute marketing to **one where humans orchestrate AI agents and AI agents execute marketing**.

The platform introduced to enable this vision is “**Agentforce Marketing”**.

At the center of Salesforce’s messaging was the phrase:

**“Agents as Marketing Multipliers”**

Salesforce does not see AI as a replacement for marketers.

Instead, the company envisions a future where marketers and AI agents work together to maximize business outcomes.

Agentforce Marketing consists of:

- Autonomous campaign execution powered by AI agents
- A reimagined content creation process centered around Content Agent
- Conversational marketing across Email, SMS, RCS, and Web channels
- Unified data powered by Data Cloud
- Personalization at customer scale
- AI agent governance and operational management

One particularly notable theme was that AI is no longer positioned merely as a content-generation tool. Instead, it is being positioned as an active participant in segmentation, campaign execution, analytics, and optimization.

Salesforce also emphasized its vision of delivering Marketing Cloud, Data Cloud, Agentforce, and Slack as a **“One Unified Platform”** rather than as separate products.

## The Agentforce Marketing Brand Realignment

At Connections 2025, **Marketing Cloud Next** was the centerpiece of Salesforce’s marketing vision. At Connections 2026, however, **Agentforce Marketing** clearly emerged as the overarching concept.

Until now, Salesforce’s Data Cloud-based marketing offerings have been known by several different names, including:

- Marketing Cloud Growth Edition
- Marketing Cloud Advanced Edition
- Marketing Cloud on Core
- Marketing Cloud Next

Salesforce has announced that the **official name of this product** will be standardized as **Marketing Cloud Next**.

Previously, Marketing Cloud Next served as an umbrella brand encompassing:

- Marketing Cloud Growth & Advanced Editions
- Salesforce Personalization
- Marketing Intelligence
- Loyalty Management

With this latest change, **Marketing Cloud Next is being positioned as the** **official product name for the Growth and Advanced editions**.

Meanwhile, the broader umbrella-brand role previously associated with Marketing Cloud Next is now being assumed by **Agentforce Marketing**.

The current product hierarchy can be summarized as follows:

**Agentforce Marketing (Brand)**  
↓  
**Marketing Cloud Next (Product)**  
↓  
**Growth Edition / Advanced Edition (Editions)**

The edition names themselves remain unchanged:

- Marketing Cloud Next Growth Edition
- Marketing Cloud Next Advanced Edition

Looking back, it was striking that the term “Marketing Cloud Next” was rarely mentioned during the keynote, while “Agentforce Marketing” was repeatedly emphasized. That alone seemed to symbolize this branding evolution.

If Connections 2025 was the event that introduced Marketing Cloud Next, then **Connections 2026 may well be remembered as the event that marked the beginning of the Agentforce Marketing era**.

Let’s now take a closer look at the innovations announced at Connections 2026 and Salesforce’s vision for the future of marketing.

## Agentforce Coworker and Slackbot

During the keynote, Salesforce highlighted both **Agentforce Coworker** and **Agentforce for Slack (Slackbot)**. These capabilities were announced prior to Connections 2026 but received significant attention during the event.

While both serve similar purposes, they are designed for different environments.

**Agentforce Coworker (Beta)** is an AI assistant accessible directly from the Salesforce search bar. Users can ask questions in natural language, invoke AI agents, and execute business actions while working inside Salesforce.

Meanwhile, **Agentforce for Slack (Slackbot)** brings similar capabilities directly into Slack. Users can interact with Agentforce, access Salesforce data, and perform Salesforce-related tasks without opening Salesforce itself.

From a marketing perspective, this can be used directly from Slack conversations to:

- Brainstorm campaign ideas
- Create emails and social media posts
- Research competitors and market trends
- Summarize customer feedback
- Manage projects and campaigns
- Analyze campaign performance
- Generate reports
- Suggest design concepts and A/B testing ideas

A simple way to think about it is:

- **Agentforce Coworker** = an AI coworker that works within Salesforce
- **Agentforce in Slack (Slackbot)**= an AI coworker that works within Slack

Salesforce’s vision is not to confine agents to the Salesforce interface, but **to bring them into the places where users already work every day**.

In other words, whether you’re working in Salesforce or collaborating in Slack, you’ll be able to leverage the same Agentforce capabilities. Salesforce is moving toward a future where AI agents are available wherever work happens.

## Headless 360: Bringing AI to Where Marketers Work

One of the most intriguing concepts announced was **Headless 360**.

Traditionally, marketers needed to access Marketing Cloud interfaces to create campaigns and manage customer journeys.

Going forward, however, Agentforce Marketing capabilities will be exposed as **MCP (Model Context Protocol) tools**, enabling marketers to access them directly from applications they already use, such as Slack.

For example, users will be able to perform tasks like:

- Creating audience segments
- Building campaigns
- Updating journeys
- Requesting performance analysis

through natural conversation in Slack.

This goes far beyond a simple Slack integration.

Salesforce is extending its customer data, business logic, and AI agent capabilities beyond Marketing Cloud and embedding them directly into marketers’ everyday workflows.

Salesforce refers to this vision as **Headless 360**, representing a shift away from a world where people must open applications to get work done and toward one where AI assistance is available wherever work happens.

## Final Thoughts

If I had to summarize Connections 2026 in a single sentence, it would be this:

**Marketing Cloud Next and Agentforce Marketing are evolving from tools that help marketers create campaigns into platforms where AI agents create content, discover customers, execute campaigns, and continuously optimize outcomes based on marketer guidance.**

With the introduction of Headless 360, these AI capabilities will no longer be confined to Marketing Cloud itself. Instead, they will become embedded directly into marketers’ daily work environments, including Slack and other tools.

During the keynote, Salesforce presenters spoke enthusiastically about this future. However, when demonstrations of Agentforce Content Agent and Marketing Goals Agent began, there was also a sense of uncertainty in the audience.

That’s understandable.

A world where AI creates content, identifies customers, launches campaigns, and autonomously optimizes results has the potential to fundamentally change how marketing works.

Many attendees were likely wondering:

- **Will traditional marketing methods still be relevant?**
- **Can we realistically keep up with a world evolving this quickly?**

I found myself asking the same questions. The pace of innovation is truly remarkable.

That said, there’s no need to chase the future all at once.

The best place to start is by understanding the fundamentals of Data 360, Agentforce, and Marketing Cloud Next — one capability at a time — and gaining hands-on experience.

Those incremental steps will likely be the most reliable way to prepare for the emerging era of AI agents.
