As a new feature released in [**Spring ’26**](/@marketingcloudtips/marketing-cloud-next-spring-26-release-highlights-24c0c804b0cb) for **Marketing Cloud Next Growth & Advanced Edition**, it is now possible to send emails with PDF attachments.

With this enhancement, marketers can directly attach and deliver important business documents such as terms and conditions, whitepapers, and product catalogs within emails.

PDF files **under 5MB that are uploaded to Salesforce CMS** can be selected and attached from the property panel on the email creation screen.

In addition, alongside the release of this feature, a **“Document” upload function** has been added to the content management screen.  
 First, upload the PDF there, and then select and attach it within the email.

## Conclusion

Emails with attachments generally tend to receive higher spam scores. In particular, for **large-scale B2C distributions**, careful attention must be paid to potential impacts on deliverability.

Also, this feature does not dynamically generate individualized PDFs. Instead, it attaches **static PDFs uploaded to Salesforce CMS**. Therefore, it is suitable for use cases such as **shared materials and policy documents**.

- **Common terms and conditions**
- **Common campaign materials**
- **Common whitepapers**
- **Common product catalogs**
- **Common price lists**

For marketing purposes, it is recommended to use link-based delivery as the default approach and limit attachments to cases where necessity is high.

Appropriately distinguishing usage depending on the purpose is the key to leveraging this new feature while maintaining deliverability.
