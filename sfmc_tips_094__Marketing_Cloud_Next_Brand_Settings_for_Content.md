In Salesforce Marketing Cloud Next Growth & Advanced Editions, when you start creating new email content, the first thing you notice is the “**Select Brand**” button displayed in the panel on the right side.

This brand feature allows companies to incorporate predefined company standards **such as “colors”, “fonts”, “button styles”, “brand identity”, and “brand tone” into email content**.

In this article, I will explain the settings that can be configured with this “**Brand**” feature.

## How to Create a New Brand

Follow these steps to create a brand in Marketing Cloud Growth & Advanced Edition:

### 1. Add a Brand in the CMS Workspace

From the Content tab, move to the Marketing Cloud workspace, click the “**Add**” button on the far right, and select “**Content**”.

### 2. Select “Brand” as the Content Type

Next, choose **“Brand”** as the content type.

### 3. Edit Brand Settings

You’ll be directed to the editing screen, where you can configure the following to ensure a consistent company style:

- **Colors**
- **Fonts**
- **Button Styles**
- **Brand Identity**
- **Brand Tone**

## Brand Details

First, in Brand Details, you configure the brand name and description, as well as the brand identity and brand tone.

This information is used when utilizing generative AI features to generate email subjects, preheaders, and email body content.

### Brand Identity

The **Brand Identity** section enables you to input a description of your company or brand. For instance, whether your company operates in logistics or retail can significantly influence the tone and content generated by the AI. Configuring this information ensures that the AI generates more relevant and effective copy.

### Brand Tone

In the **Brand Tone** section, you can set the tone of voice used by the generative AI. While the default tone is professional, you can choose from the following five options, and make fine adjustments as needed to align with your brand. Instructions can be provided in natural language, as shown in the example below:

- **Professional**
- **Casual**
- **Urgent**
- **Inquisitive**
- **Plain**

These settings help the AI consistently create content aligned with your brand identity and tone.

### Colors

This section is useful for modifying default settings such as text colors and background colors.

### Typography

Configure font types and sizes here. For email content, it’s recommended to maintain a base font size of 16px and ensure a minimum size of 12px. The rem value represents a percentage based on the base font size being set to 1.

Additionally, you can fine-tune text styles to match your design preferences.

### Buttons

Adjust button styles — such as color and shape — to align with your brand image. By default, the button has a blue background.

### Margin and Padding

Margin and padding significantly impact the appearance of your emails. While these settings are ideally configured while previewing, if you frequently use presets (large, medium, small), it’s recommended to define them in advance.

### Borders

Set the thickness and corner rounding of borders to suit your design.

## How to Configure a Default Brand

You can set a default brand for the Marketing Cloud workspace, **automatically assign the brand**, and save time.

1. Open the workspace, and from the settings button, select “**Default Brand**”.

2. Configure the brand and save it.

3. After configuring it, the brand will automatically be assigned to new content.

## Final Thoughts

Leveraging **Brand Settings** allows you to standardize the appearance and tone of your email content, improving efficiency, especially when reusing similar designs frequently.

***Note:*** *Even if you update the contents of a brand after content has already been published, the contents of the already published content will not change. To apply the updates, unpublish the content once and then publish it again.*
