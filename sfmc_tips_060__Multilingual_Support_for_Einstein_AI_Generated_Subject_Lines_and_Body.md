One year ago, with the Winter ’24 release, Salesforce introduced a feature that automatically generates “email subject lines” and “email body drafts” based on your organization’s brand identity and other factors.

Previously, while you could input “requests” in Japanese etc, the “response” was returned in English. However, with the Winter ’25 release, the new multi-language support allows the feature to be used in **French, German, Italian, Japanese, Portuguese, and Spanish**.

In this post, I’ll explore how this feature feels when used in my native language, Japanese.

### **Setup**

This feature is called **Einstein Generative AI**, and you need to enable it in the **Einstein Copy Insights setup** to use it.

In the setup screen, you can configure “**Brand Settings**”.

To create content efficiently while maintaining brand consistency and deepening relationships with customers, marketers must define their **Brand Identity** and **Brand Personality**.

The **Brand Identity** shapes the impression your organization gives to customers. Setting the appropriate personality ensures that the generated content consistently reflects your brand’s voice and tone, leading to a more cohesive customer experience.

The **Brand Personality** refers to the style set to express the brand’s characteristics. Two standard personalities, “Professional” and “Casual”, are available. Additionally, you can create up to 10 custom personalities based on your organization’s needs.

When generating content, the configured personality will automatically be reflected, ensuring the message aligns with your brand.

### **Examples of Custom Personalities**

According to the help documentation, you can set personalities like:

- **Urgent**: When conveying urgency, this personality emphasizes deadlines or actions without overusing capital letters or exclamation marks, ensuring the message remains reliable. Example: “Please take action now. The deadline is approaching”.
- **Inquisitive**: This personality sparks curiosity by asking questions related to the reader’s interests and offering answers, encouraging engagement. Example: “What are you curious about right now? Check it out!”

Of course, you can stick to just using the standard “Professional” and “Casual” personalities. It might be helpful to start by trying these out to see how the content is generated before diving into customization.

### **Hands-on Test**

Now, let’s test this feature by generating an “email subject line”.

Here’s what I input in the request screen:

- **Important message**: 冬に発売となる新作メニューを紹介したい。4つの商品があります。I want to introduce four new menu items launching this winter.
- **Brand personality**: Professional
- **Sample subject line (optional)**: 冬の新作メニューをご紹介 Introducing Our New Winter Menu

After checking the disclaimer box and clicking the “Create” button, five draft email subject lines were displayed on the right, all in Japanese:

1. 冬の新作メニューを一足先にチェック！4つの新商品を紹介
2. 冬の味覚がここに！新作メニューをお見逃しなく
3. 新しい冬のメニューが登場！あなたのお気に入りはどれ？
4. 冬限定！新作メニュー 4 品を今すぐご覧ください
5. 冬の新作メニューを楽しもう！4 つの魅力的な商品を紹介

For reference, the image below shows the results before the feature supported multiple languages, when responses were returned in English:

1. Exciting New Winter Menu Launch — Four Delicious Additions!
2. Warm up this Winter with Our Four New Menu Items!
3. Introducing Our Four Winter Menu Must-Tries!
4. Four Mouth-Watering New Menu Items for the Winter Season!
5. Winter Just Got Better with Our Four New Menu Offerings!

### **AI Content Monitoring with the New Audit Export Feature**

Another feature released in Winter ’25 allows you to audit the usage history of generated subject lines and body content. This can be exported as a CSV file **from the Einstein Copy Insights setup screen**, allowing you to ensure content adheres to your brand’s guidelines.

The Japanese output generated by this feature is crafted with more attention to detail to resonate with local audiences.

Additionally, you’ll notice that the quality of the generated content is significantly higher than the sample I inputted, “Introducing Our New Winter Menu”. I encourage you to take full advantage of this feature!
